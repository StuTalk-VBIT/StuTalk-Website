MAKING FREINDS

  • P. Laasyakshara
  • Class of 2026

Making friends did not always come naturally to me, and introverts who are reading this may feel the same way. But the whole point of an Engineering degree is to make friends and build connections. Trust me, you are not going to make it alone. Good friends do not make you feel weird about your quirks or interests. You don’t have to pretend to be something you are not. They might not be the ones you hang out with every day but they certainly are the ones you go to when you are in trouble. The thing is, it is not always easy to make friends. The loneliness that comes with it can be pretty devastating. Being around people who don't understand you is tremendously isolating, which can lead to a lot of self-doubt and despair.



I recall there were days in my first year when I didn't want to come to college because I thought there was no one I could talk to. Good friends do not necessarily imply having a ton of friends. It could be just one person who makes your college pretty bearable. Remember that you don’t always need to make friends for life. There will be people in your life who will only stay for a short time and there will be people who you can not wait to get rid of (I certainly know the ones I will be unfollowing right after graduation).



So get out there, talk to people, figure out your likes and dislikes. When you meet people who bring out the sense of humour you didn't even know you had, every thing will be worthwhile.



“Each friend represents a world in us, a world not born until they arrive, and it's only by this meeting that a new world is born.” – Anais Nin
