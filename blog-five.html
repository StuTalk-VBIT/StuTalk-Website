Women – Victims for life

  • Revanth Thoutam
  • Class of 2023

While the entire world is busy in tracing out the pandemic and its influence on daily life, did you stop to think of what women are facing all throughout this crisis? While some were having the best times of their lives with their families, others are suffering in the darkness behind closed doors hoping for them to open so that they can breathe. Even before Edison invented light, women around us made our lives brighter, even God himself is incomplete without his woman.

They sacrifice their passion for their families and hide all their pain and struggles in them just like a pot being filled with water but every pot overflows with too much water in it. No matter how much this world has evolved and became civilized, women were given the least priority, freedom to them was seen as a taboo in our culture.

The lockdown has triggered a rise in violence against women like never before in the past 10 years.

In just Haryana, there were more than 66 rapes cases reported, 142 molestation reports, and 117 cases of dowry abuse reports, and now just think about it on a nationwide scale. In many households, women are being victimized by their husbands have no chance but to bear the abuse.

The empty roads are not what they seem to be, there are well disguised to trap innocent girls to become victims and get ruined. It has always been the same; women are always the casualties either in love, family, society, monsters lurking around, or of their responsibilities that they are compelled to take up.

Well now is the time to think about this issue, not just to help them but also to make a greater chance for the women that are struggling to raise their voice. We live in a society where many gods and kings are recognized after their mother’s name. It’s not the police, the government or the society; it’s the thoughts and age-old perspective of people where the change should start, children should be taught from a young age on how a woman faces the world and put them on a righteous path. It's not just the men but also the women that need to have a greater understanding and stronger support for their fellow women. Let us give ourselves a thought for a change for the greater good of all the mothers and sisters around us.
