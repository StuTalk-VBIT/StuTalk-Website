The impact of “Gulag Archipelago”

  • John Saurabh
  • Class of 2022
"Literature becomes the living memory of a nation" - Aleksandr Solzhenitsyn

In a world flooding with drastic revolutions and larger than life discoveries, it is vital to recognize the impact of literature on the masses.

"The Gulag Archipelago" written by Aleksandr Solzhenitsyn who was a Russian Novelist, historian and a fearless critic of the Soviet Union exposed the harsh realities of the forced war labor camps known as Gulag in Russia. Gulag which included a vast number of labor camps in Russia was the crux of all the evils begotten by Communism.

Aleksandr became a political prisoner of the Gulag and during his time in the camp, he wrote his experience and those of his fellow mates unto harsh extends. The plight of helpless people subjected to these camps were horrifying to imagine and disheartening to dwell upon. He described them from his perspective not as an incidental phenomenon but as the very nature of such a philosophy called communism.

The crimes committed against children and women in those camps were described unto excruciating detail. Anyone ruffled through its page could feel their suffering. The crimes committed against the people in Gulag were not to be confined to the country's history books but he believed them to be heinous crimes against humanity itself.

The book "Gulag Archipelago" after being smuggled out of Soviet Union became tremendously popular as it stressed the importance of Human Rights to all sections of people. It led to amendment of Human Right policies in few countries. The evils of communism were debunked which eventually led to the downfall of such a belief. The literary work of one touched millions and moved nations, his account of events in the camp revealed the harsh realities and the price they paid to earn the bare minimum. He was expelled from the country and the book was banned which only made his book more influential. He was later recognized for his work

We, who have received the gift of freedom must remember and revere the ones who paid a huge price to create it.
