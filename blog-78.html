A STURDY WOMAN

  • B. Bindu Rao
  • Class of 2025

In a small village with little to no proper facilities, lived a girl named Rekha, the youngest of three siblings and faced numerous difficulties. Rekha's mother died when she was six, and her grandmother cared for them briefly before her father remarried to provide better care for his children. Rekha went to school till the fourth grade but had to stop due to financial difficulties, despite her desire to continue her education. She started working to support her family, giving most of her earnings to her father and saving a tiny bit for herself.


Rekha's attachment to her brother was strong, he looked after her like a mother, especially when she went to bed hungry. When she was of age, her father arranged her marriage, hoping it would bring her peace. But, her new life was filled with abuse and dowry demands from her husband and in-laws. Despite her many pleas, her parents did not support her. This led to her facing the abuse and hardships in her marriage on her own.

Rekha's husband did not work. So she was determined to provide a better future for her daughter, Rekha worked tirelessly despite her difficult circumstances. She lived in a rented house and often went hungry just so her daughter was well-fed and educated. Rekha was delighted to see her daughter excel academically, a testament to her unwavering work ethic.


Rekha's story serves as an example of perseverance and the strength of self-belief. It highlights the challenges that women faced for many years just for the sake of their families and children.

Finally, I want you all to never give up, be strong, be passionate, work hard, believe in yourself, and be confident. No matter what, you will succeed in your life.
